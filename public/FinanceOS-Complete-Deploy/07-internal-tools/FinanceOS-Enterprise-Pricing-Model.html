Negotiation Playbook
Tested talk tracks and tactics for common objections during enterprise pricing discussions.
Do — Winning Moves
  • Lead with transparent pricing — "We publish our rates because we believe in earning your trust"
  • Anchor on total cost of ownership — include their current SI/consultant costs in the comparison
  • Offer committed spend discounts instead of one-off discounts — locks in revenue, customer feels they earned it
  • Bundle xP&A departments at 3+ for automatic 15% module discount — drives expansion
  • Waive implementation fee on 2yr+ commits — removes objection, costs us $7.5K, earns $50K+ LTV
  • Give SSO/SCIM free on any enterprise deal — it's table stakes, don't let it become a negotiation point
  • Use "migration from Anaplan" as a competitive displacement discount trigger — 15% automatic
  • Offer 60-day POC with 3 power seats free — prove value before asking for commitment
Don't — Common Mistakes
  • Never discount more than 10% without getting something back (longer term, case study, reference)
  • Don't compete on price alone against Vena — they're cheaper. Compete on AI and real-time capabilities
  • Don't trash Planful's Analyst Assistant — acknowledge it, then differentiate on native AI vs bolt-on
  • Don't let procurement stretch you below floor price — walk away and come back in 30 days
  • Never give multi-year pricing on a month-to-month contract — committed spend requires committed term
  • Don't offer custom connectors free — they cost us $5K to build. Charge at least $49/mo
  • Avoid discussing ARR targets ($48.6M) in sales calls — it's investor info, not prospect info
  • Don't discount viewer seats — they're already cheap and high-margin. Discount platform base instead
