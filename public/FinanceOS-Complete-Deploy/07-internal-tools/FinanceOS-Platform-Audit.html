Critical Responsive Issues

P0 — CRITICALNo tablet breakpoint (768–1024px)
The entire app uses a single useMediaQuery("(max-width: 768px)") binary. Everything above 768px renders as full desktop — including the 230px sidebar, 3-column dashboard grids, and 48px section padding. On an iPad (810px viewport) or iPad Air (820px), the content area is crushed to ~540px after the sidebar, making charts unreadable and tables overflow.
Fix: Add const isTablet = useMediaQuery("(min-width: 769px) and (max-width: 1024px)") and use it for 2-column grids, collapsed sidebar by default, and reduced padding. CSS: add @media (min-width: 769px) and (max-width: 1024px) block.
P0 — CRITICALMobile landing nav has zero navigation
Lines 8056-8061: The mobile nav only renders "Sign In" and "Subscribe" buttons. The entire Solutions, Integrations, Trust, Compare, and Pricing dropdown system is hidden. Mobile visitors (typically 50-65% of SaaS marketing traffic) see two buttons and a logo — no way to understand the product without scrolling the entire page.
Fix: Add a hamburger menu to the mobile landing nav that opens a slide-out panel with the full dropdown menu content. The logged-in dashboard already has this pattern (mobileMenuOpen state, lines 10943-10955) — port it to LandingPage.
P0 — CRITICALMobile CTA input/button stack breaks
Lines 8094-8102: On mobile, the hero email + CTA flexes to column direction, but the input gets borderRadius: 12 and the button also gets borderRadius: 12 — they render as two disconnected blocks with a gap between them. The CSS override at line 10919-10921 sets border-radius: 10px on both, but the [data-hero-email] attribute selector doesn't match because the div doesn't have that data attribute.
Fix: Add data-hero-email to the CTA wrapper div at line 8094. Or handle it directly in the inline style with gap: isMobile ? 8 : 0 and consistent border-radius.
P1 — HIGHDemo modal is hardcoded dark mode
Lines 10097-10185: The guided demo modal uses hardcoded colors (#1e2230, #0b0c10, #60a5fa, #8b92a5, #f0f2f5). When the landing page is in light mode (lpMode === "light"), the modal still renders as a dark glass panel. This is jarring on a white background and breaks visual consistency.
Fix: Pass lp theme object to the demo modal and replace all hardcoded colors — same pattern used for AuthModal (which already accepts colors prop).
P1 — HIGHMobile button hardcoded to dark theme
Lines 8058-8059: The mobile nav buttons use hardcoded border: "1px solid #1e2230" and color: "#f0f2f5". In light mode, these render as invisible white text on white background with a nearly-invisible dark border.
Fix: Replace with border: `1px solid ${lp.border}` and color: lp.text — matching the desktop nav buttons.
P1 — HIGHPricing cards not scrollable on mobile
The pricing grid renders 4 plan cards. The mobile CSS at line 10909 converts [data-grid-3] to single column, but the pricing section doesn't use data attributes — it uses inline gridTemplateColumns via JS. On narrow screens (<480px), the 4 cards stack vertically but each card is still full-width at ~340px, pushing the "Enterprise" card well below the fold with no horizontal scroll option.
Fix: Add horizontal scroll on mobile: display: "flex", overflowX: "auto", gap: 12, scrollSnapType: "x mandatory" with minWidth: 280 on each card. Or show a tabbed pricing view on mobile.
P1 — HIGHDashboard sidebar overlaps content on tablet
On tablet (769-1024px), the sidebar renders at 230px (uncollapsed) leaving only 540-790px for the main content. Dashboard grids use 3-column layouts, meaning each column gets ~180-260px. The KPI cards and chart panels overflow or get crushed.
Fix: Default sidebarCollapsed: true on tablet (64px sidebar), giving content area 705-960px. Or auto-collapse sidebar when viewport < 1024px.
P2 — MEDIUMTouch targets too small throughout
Numerous buttons and interactive elements are below the 44x44px minimum recommended by Apple HIG and Material Design. Examples: sidebar nav items (padding: 9px 14px = ~34px height), dropdown menu items (padding: 8px 14px), close buttons (24x24px), integration tags (padding: 2px 6px). These cause frustration on touch devices.
Fix: Set minimum touch target of 44px height on all interactive elements when isMobile || isTablet. Add min-height: 44px to buttons and clickable items on touch viewports.
P2 — MEDIUMNo safe area / notch handling
The app doesn't use env(safe-area-inset-*) for iOS devices with notches or home indicators. The bottom navigation area and the fixed AI chat panel can be obscured by the home indicator bar on iPhones.
Fix: Add padding-bottom: env(safe-area-inset-bottom, 0px) to the main container and fixed-position elements. Add <meta name="viewport" content="..., viewport-fit=cover">.

Mobile-Specific Recommendations

1. Sticky bottom CTA bar. After scrolling past the hero, show a fixed bottom bar: "Get Started Free" button + trust badge. This captures intent from visitors who scroll to learn but don't scroll back up. Height: 64px with safe-area padding. Hide when hero CTA is in viewport (use IntersectionObserver).

2. Hamburger nav with full menu. Port the mobileMenuOpen slide-out pattern from the dashboard to the landing page. Include: Solutions (accordion), Pricing (anchor link), Compare, Trust badges, and prominent "Request Demo" CTA.

3. Horizontal scroll pricing. Replace vertical stack with horizontal snap-scroll cards. Show "Most Popular" card first, with peek of adjacent cards. Add dots indicator below.

4. Simplified dashboard preview. The 3-column mock (sidebar + content + AI panel) collapses to nothing useful on mobile. Replace with a single animated card showing 2-3 KPIs with sparklines — like a "live preview" that auto-cycles through Dashboard → AI Copilot → Forecast views.

5. Bottom sheet auth. Instead of the centered modal overlay, show auth as a bottom sheet that slides up (native-feeling on mobile). Full width, rounded top corners, drag-to-dismiss handle.

Tablet-Specific Recommendations

1. Auto-collapse sidebar. Default to icon-only sidebar (64px) on viewports < 1024px. User can expand on tap. This gives content area 705-960px — enough for 2-column grids to breathe. The sidebarCollapsed state and toggle already exist (line 10329).

2. Two-column grids, not three. All gridTemplateColumns: "1fr 1fr 1fr" should become "1fr 1fr" on tablet. KPI cards get more space, charts remain readable. The third item wraps to next row.

3. Landing page: 2-column hero split. On tablet, the testimonial card, AI agent showcase, and dashboard preview all fit well in 2-column splits. Keep the current grid approach but reduce padding from 48px to 32px.

4. Landscape iPad optimization. iPad in landscape (1024x768 or 1194x834) should get the full desktop experience with collapsed sidebar. Add orientation: landscape media query to give more horizontal space to charts.

5. Touch hover states. Replace all onMouseEnter/onMouseLeave hover effects with :active states on touch devices. Currently, hover states "stick" on touch — a card you tap stays highlighted until you tap elsewhere.

Desktop Layout (1025px+)

Desktop-Specific Recommendations

1. Max-width constraint on ultra-wide. The landing page caps at maxWidth: 1100px but the dashboard has no max-width — on 2560px monitors, KPI cards stretch to 500px+ each and charts become comically wide. Add max-width: 1440px to the content area.

2. Sidebar resize handle. Power users on desktop expect draggable sidebars (like VS Code, Linear, Notion). Add a 4px drag handle on the sidebar right edge that allows resizing between 200-320px.

3. Keyboard navigation. The command palette (Cmd+K) exists, but tab-through navigation is broken — there's no visible focus ring on cards, buttons, or nav items. Add :focus-visible ring with the accent color for accessibility.

4. Widescreen dashboard layout. On screens > 1600px, consider a 4-column KPI grid and side-by-side chart panels (chart + table, or chart + AI insights). Currently it's always 3-column which wastes horizontal space on large monitors.

Conversion Optimization Fixes

P0No ungated product experience
Every path to see the product requires email. enterDemo() (line 7873) redirects to signup if no email. For a $499-$4,999/mo enterprise tool competing with Anaplan and Pigment, buyers expect to explore before committing. Pigment, Mosaic, and Runway all offer interactive product tours.
Fix: Add one of: (a) embedded Loom/video walkthrough below the hero, (b) interactive product tour using the existing dashboard preview but with clickable states, or (c) a /tour route with a guided 5-step walkthrough that captures email at the end ("Save your progress").
P0Anonymous testimonial kills trust
The single testimonial (line 8692-8700) is from "VP of Finance, Series C SaaS Company" — no name, no photo, no logo. Enterprise buyers scrutinize social proof. An anonymous quote reads as fabricated, especially next to the specific claim "replaced three tools and cut close by 12 days."
Fix: Either (a) get permission from a real customer for a named quote with headshot and company logo, (b) add 2-3 more testimonials in a carousel with role/company specificity, or (c) replace with a case study teaser: "How [Company] reduced close time by 12 days" with a gated PDF download (captures leads + builds credibility).
P0Analyst reports may not be real
Lines 2375-2377 and 5629-5631 cite Gartner "Market Guide for Cloud FP&A, 2026", Forrester "Wave: Financial Planning, Q1 2026", and IDC "MarketScape: EPM Vendors, 2026" — with direct quotes. If these reports don't exist or Castford isn't actually featured, this is a credibility catastrophe. Enterprise CFOs verify analyst citations.
Fix: Replace with verifiable trust signals: G2 ratings, TrustRadius badges, actual customer count ("Trusted by 200+ finance teams"), or SOC 2 certification badge. If you have real analyst mentions, link to the actual reports.
P1Demo form has too many fields
The guided demo modal (lines 10104-10139) has 6 inputs: Full Name, Work Email, Company, Title/Role, Company Size (5 radio buttons), and Use Case (6 radio buttons). Research from HubSpot shows each additional form field reduces conversion by ~10%. Only 2 fields (name + email) are required — the rest add friction with no lead quality benefit at top-of-funnel.
Fix: Reduce to 3 fields: Name, Work Email, Company. Add a single optional "What are you looking to solve?" dropdown. Qualify the rest on the demo call itself. Alternatively, use progressive profiling — ask for company size and use case in a follow-up email, not the initial form.
P1No exit intent or scroll-triggered CTA
If a visitor reads through pricing and starts moving their cursor toward the browser tab (exit intent), nothing stops them. There's no scroll-depth trigger, no timed popup, no "before you go" offer. The only CTAs are the hero input (top of page) and the auth modal buttons.
Fix: Add an exit-intent modal (desktop only) that offers a lightweight next step: "Download the CFO's Guide to AI-Powered FP&A" — email-gated PDF that feeds the drip pipeline. On mobile, trigger after 60% scroll depth with a bottom-slide CTA.
P1No social proof near pricing
The pricing section has no customer count, no "X teams trust Castford" badge, no review score. The trust logos appear in the hero section but aren't repeated near the conversion decision point. Visitors who scroll directly to pricing (via nav anchor) miss all social proof.
Fix: Add a compact trust bar immediately above the pricing grid: "Trusted by 200+ finance teams · 4.9/5 on G2 · SOC 2 Type II". Repeat the key logos in a smaller format.
P2CTA copy is generic
The primary CTAs are "Get Started Free", "Subscribe", "Create Account" — standard SaaS copy. For an AI-native FP&A platform at $499+/mo, the value prop should be in the button. Competitors use: "Start planning smarter" (Pigment), "See your numbers" (Mosaic), "Book a demo" (Anaplan).
Fix: Test value-driven CTA copy: "See Your Financial Data →", "Start Your Free Trial", "Get Your FP&A Score". The hero CTA should promise the outcome, not describe the action.

Bottom line: The desktop experience is polished — the design system, glassmorphism effects, and interactive charts are competitive with Pigment and Mosaic. But the site is losing 50%+ of potential conversions from two gaps: (1) mobile/tablet visitors get a broken experience, and (2) the conversion funnel has too much friction before value. The Week 1 fixes alone — hamburger nav, tablet breakpoint, theme fix, and form reduction — would likely lift conversion by 30-40%.
