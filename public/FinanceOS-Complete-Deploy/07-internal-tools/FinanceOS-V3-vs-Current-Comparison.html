Part 1 — Visual Design

Design hierarchy, polish, and clarity

Every section of the landing page was redesigned to meet competitive SaaS benchmarks set by Linear, Vercel, and Ramp.

Navigation

Flat link bar

Solutions, Integrations, Trust, Pricing, Compare, Investors in a flat row. "Subscribe" as primary CTA. Dark mode toggle icon. No visual hierarchy between primary and secondary nav items.

Solid background with no depth separation on scroll. All nav items carry equal visual weight.

Frosted-glass sticky nav with clear CTA hierarchy

Platform, Solutions, Integrations, Pricing, Resources with muted secondary weight. "Sign in" as a subtle text link. "Start Free" as the sole green primary CTA. Frosted glass effect creates depth separation on scroll, keeping content visible behind the nav.

Frosted glass nav with transparency and blur. Clear visual separation between informational links and the primary conversion action.
Hero Section

"Financial planning that thinks before it answers"

Headline with teal italic emphasis on "thinks before." Long sub-copy explaining ERP/CRM/billing connection. Email input field + "Get Started Free" button. Two secondary CTAs below ("Request a Guided Demo" + "Sign In to Explore"). Smart alerts status dot.

Conversion flow: email field, primary button, 2 extra actions, plus status indicators. Issue: 4 competing actions create decision paralysis. Requiring an email upfront adds friction for top-of-funnel visitors.

"Financial planning that earns 5-star reviews"

Announcement badge ("Now available: xP&A across 7 departments" + NEW tag). Large responsive headline with tight letter-spacing. Clean 2-button layout: "Start Free Trial" (green primary) + "Book a Demo" (outline). Single trust line. Then a full interactive dashboard mockup — sidebar, 4 KPI cards, bar chart — acting as visual proof of the product.

Conversion flow: announcement, headline, subtext, 2 clear actions, trust note, then product visual. Reduces decision points from 4 to 2. Dashboard visual replaces abstract claims with product evidence.
Social Proof

No visible logo strip

The current production page does not include a company logo strip or "Trusted by" section in the above-the-fold or near-fold area. Social proof relies solely on feature claims and status indicators.

8-company trust strip + 5-metric dark band

Immediately after the hero: "Trusted by finance teams at Figma, Notion, Vercel, Linear, Ramp, Mercury, Retool, Webflow." Followed by a dark metrics band showing $48.6M ARR, 118% NDR, 2,400+ Teams, 0.8x Burn Multiple, 84.7% Gross Margin in a data-focused format.

Pattern: brand trust strip followed by operating metric proof. Leads with recognizable company names before presenting any feature pitch — standard for enterprise SaaS.
Feature Presentation

Icon row at page bottom

Feature icons visible at the bottom of the viewport. Features appear to be presented further down the page in a conventional card list format. No visual density or emphasis hierarchy between capabilities.

6-card bento grid with span-2 emphasis

Asymmetric grid layout (3-column, 2-row). AI Copilot and Integrations get double-width emphasis cards with large KPI stats. Close, xP&A, Scenarios, Compliance get standard single cards. Each card features an animated mini-chart or performance metric. Cards fade in as visitors scroll down.

Primary capabilities (AI, Integrations) receive 2x visual weight. Every card follows a consistent structure: category label, title, description, and a supporting data point or chart. Hover states provide interactive feedback.
AI Copilot Showcase

Headline-level claim only

"…thinks before it answers" + "AI-powered variance detection, natural language querying, and visible reasoning" in sub-copy. No interactive or visual proof of how the AI actually works.

Split layout: feature list + live terminal demo

Left column: "Your senior analyst, available 24/7" with 4 capability cards (Variance Analysis, Anomaly Detection, Proactive Insights, Multi-Turn Conversations). Right column: terminal-style mockup showing a real AI conversation — a CFO asks "Why did OPEX exceed budget by $340K?" and the AI responds with 3 sourced factors (Eng headcount +$180K, AWS infra +$95K, Legal +$65K), then offers to reforecast Q2-Q4.

This is the single most persuasive element. It shifts from "we have AI" to "here's exactly what our AI does with your data."
Typography & Hierarchy

Standard weight distribution

Headline and body text use conventional sizing. Teal color accent provides some visual differentiation but limited typographic hierarchy between sections.

8-level type scale with negative tracking

Headlines use extra-bold weight with tight spacing for impact. Section headers step down in size but keep the same family. Category labels are small uppercase with wide spacing. Financial metrics use a data-focused monospace font for clarity. 5 distinct text color tiers separate headings, body copy, captions, and muted labels.

Two font families: DM Sans for all marketing copy, JetBrains Mono for financial data and metrics. Consistent hierarchy from page hero down to footer links.
Part 2 — Bi-Directional Function

Two-way interaction, not just reading

V3 introduces interactive pathways where users both consume content and send signals back — creating engagement loops that the current page lacks entirely.

01

Product Preview in Hero

The hero section includes a full dashboard mockup with interactive sidebar items (Dashboard, P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow, etc.). Visitors see the actual product before signing up — building confidence in the platform and reducing perceived risk of starting a trial.

Visitor sees product -- product builds trust
02

AI Copilot Demonstration

The AI Copilot section reveals a realistic conversation as visitors scroll into view. The content shows an actual variance analysis (OPEX budget overrun with sourced line items), turning passive visitors into engaged readers who understand what the AI can do for their finance team.

Visitor scrolls -- AI capability becomes visible
03

Interactive Feature Cards

Every capability card responds to cursor interaction with a subtle lift and depth effect, inviting visitors to explore. The mini-charts and KPI stats within each card create a data-driven, living feel — reinforcing that FinanceOS is built around real financial metrics, not just marketing copy.

Visitor explores -- features respond visually
04

Dual Conversion Paths

Every major section offers two clear next steps: a primary action (Start Free Trial) and a secondary action (Book Demo / Talk to Sales). This captures both self-serve buyers who want to try immediately and enterprise prospects who prefer a guided sales conversation.

Visitor decides -- two paths to conversion
05

Integration Recognition Strip

The integration section displays branded connector chips for NetSuite, SAP, Salesforce, Stripe, and others. Visitors immediately recognize the tools their finance team already uses — creating an instant "they connect to our stack" moment that plain text mentions cannot deliver.

Visitor recognizes their tools -- trust increases
06

Progressive Content Reveal

Page sections fade in as visitors scroll, creating a guided experience rather than a static wall of text. Content reveals progressively, rewarding engagement with new information and encouraging deeper exploration of the platform's capabilities.

Visitor scrolls deeper -- more value revealed
